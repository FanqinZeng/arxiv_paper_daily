I am interested in embodied agents, especially agents that combine perception, language understanding, task planning, and physical action in simulated or real robotic environments.

My main focus is on embodied Agent systems for robotics. I want to track papers about Vision-Language-Action models, World Action Models, world models for embodied AI, long-horizon task planning, robot manipulation, mobile manipulation, humanoid agents, and generalist robot policies.

I am especially interested in:
1. Embodied Agent architectures that connect LLMs/VLMs with robot control policies.
2. Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robotic manipulation, mobile manipulation, and generalist robot control.
3. World Action Models (WAMs), embodied world models, action-conditioned video prediction, and future imagination for robot planning.
4. On-Policy Distillation (OPD), VLA post-training, offline-to-online robot learning, interactive post-training, and self-improving embodied agents.
5. Long-horizon embodied task planning, hierarchical planning, skill decomposition, replanning, and closed-loop execution.
6. Affordance learning, 3D scene understanding, open-vocabulary embodied perception, object-centric perception, and part-level manipulation.
7. Robot learning datasets, teleoperation data, simulation-to-real transfer, synthetic environments, and benchmark evaluation.
8. Real-world deployment of embodied agents, including low-latency inference, on-device VLA models, ROS2 integration, safety, and robust policy evaluation.

I prefer papers that provide:
1. New architectures for embodied agents.
2. New VLA or WAM models.
3. Methods that improve policy learning, post-training, robustness, or generalization.
4. Strong experiments on embodied benchmarks such as LIBERO, RoboTwin, CALVIN, RLBench, ManiSkill, Habitat, AI2-THOR, ALFRED, BEHAVIOR, Meta-World, RoboSuite, or real robots.
5. Open-source code, datasets, pretrained models, or reproducible benchmarks.

I am less interested in:
1. Pure large language model papers without embodiment, robotics, action, or environment interaction.
2. Pure computer vision papers that do not connect to embodied perception or robot action.
3. Pure reinforcement learning theory without robot or embodied agent experiments.
4. Medical imaging, autonomous driving-only VLA, recommendation systems, or finance agents unless the method is clearly transferable to embodied agents.
